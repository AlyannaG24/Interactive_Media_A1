Luni

Interactive Media: Assignment 1: Interactive Experience Research Project

Alyanna Gaw (s4004022)

The first thing I saw was the visual design of the website. I thought the way it incorporated soft color palettes with 3D objects, paired with well-polished and creative design elements made it very different from an average business website. Instead, I felt more like I was experiencing an interactive design experience. Even though I loved its aesthetics, I did find that some of the moving animations were slightly distracting from the overall experience and focus. My overall perception based on all aspects mentioned above is that it is an exciting and unique site.
I spent the first 2 minutes on the website:
  • Accessed the main Luni page.
  • First noticed both the landing page formats and animation
  • Saw that the landing page contained a lot of large font and motion/3D graphics.
  • Continued to observe the whole page without performing any actions.
  • Moved to navigate through the left side/menu bar of the page.
  • Proceeded to the Omada sections.
  • Examined the images and text found on the Omada section page.
  • Began scrolling down through the Omada page.
  • Noticed different sections each with large text, an image, pantone colour, etc.
  • Continued to scroll down on the Omada page to look at more sections of the page.
  • Reviewed the lower sections on the Omada page.
  • Moved to another section of the website called "Studio"; looked at image and text.
  • Scrolled down through the "Studio" section using the same method described above.
  • Looked at multiple images, multiple blocks of text, and multiple formats.
  • Moved on to scan the rest of the website, reading as needed while scanning.
I spent most of my time with the scrolling sections of the Omada and Studio pages. The homepage only occupied a small amount of my time, since after my first impression I turned my focus to a broader exploration of the entire site. The majority of my time was spent scrolling through the pages to see how the visuals and text worked together as one cohesive way to get involved with the content. My strongest interaction with the site was through its visual storytelling components, like large text, imagery, animated components, and page designs. Even when certain aspects were challenging to read, that did not prevent me from continuing to focus on those elements.
The primary behaviour that I experienced in this interaction was scrolling. I would scroll from page to page to see what was next and reveal additional content. This site seems very heavily reliant on scrolling as a method for exploration rather than having the user conduct lots of clicks or complete a certain task. When I scrolled, I would get another section to load with text, images or advertisements. The overall experience flowed better and was much more visual because of the scrolling. I think the website is guiding me through at a slower pace, encouraging me to take in each section before moving onto the next one, rather than simply trying to find one specific piece of information quickly.
My understanding is that Luni's website's primary purpose is to showcase its brand as being creative/innovative while also promoting both its products and its studio identity. The website works toward creating the impression for users that Luni is modern, creative and design-driven. I believe the company's intention is to provide an experience that makes the visitor want to continue investigating Luni as a company and that will allow them to remember the brand from the unique, visually appealing experience they had.
The website's objectives are primarily conveyed through visual design. There are several large and bold visual aspects employed on the site with many aesthetic choices, including bright dreamy colours, three-dimensional objects, animated content, and oversized typefaces, which help create an impression of creativity and innovation. The movement between multiple different elements allowed me to interact with them and to appreciate each element as a carefully crafted piece of work, thus giving the impression that Luni is a high-quality brand with original ideas. Overall, the website's design communicates that Luni places significant emphasis on creative expression and an aesthetically pleasing presentation.

I believe the site is intended to be used primarily how it is meant to be used; that is, for short periods of time but possibly repeated use over time. For example, while I could see a user being on the website for an extended amount of time (i.e., more than 90 minutes) if they were very interested in a certain section of the website (such as one of its brands) or if they were using the website for an in-depth look at the various parts of the website, but otherwise, it really appears to be used in short, several minutes, bursts.

Since most of the way through the website is heavily designed and created with images, it also seems to be designed to allow for several visitations to it. Therefore, I would imagine someone will first go to the website to get an idea about the website and brand, then comeback and visit again to check out a different section or information, and then visit again to see information they had previously viewed or visited.

The website gives off this sense of having many different places/reasons to keep coming back based on its overall layout and design. Each area has its own page, so you can individually look at everything; this means you will have many things you can give attention to when you come back versus looking back at just one after finishing the site all together (like you do with traditional sites). Additionally, due to the nature of using images primarily and scrolling mode to interact with content, it gives off a feeling of understanding that you'll browse around a little bit, leave, and hopefully come back sometime in the future.

There aren't many ways within the site itself that would help Encourage you to return, however, I think that the large amount of rich media, content and animations as well as having multiple areas of the site which allows the user multiple options to create reasons for using that site again later.

The site reminded me of multiple types of media. It had a 3D feel like an interactive digital art/design portfolio, as well as the page layouts seemed to borrow from magazine/editorial design, with large type and heavily image-based. Certain parts also seemed more like a brand's ad campaign than something simply providing factual material; i.e., they focused on the mood and identity of a brand rather than facts about the brand. The use of motion & visuals also gave it a slight feeling of being in a game environment or digital exhibition. Overall, the site appears to be drawn from creative media rather than traditional business-oriented web sites.
From these media references, I have a sense that I will be encouraged to look closely at the website and study its design visually instead of only textually. The website resembles an exhibition or portfolio of design work and appears to have been designed to encourage users to browse and observe rather than rush. I felt that I was expected to explore, scroll, and absorb everything around me on this website, without needing to complete any immediate, specific tasks. Instead, I felt that I should enjoy my time on this site while experiencing it.
This site created a sense of being curious, intrigued, and impressed for me. The creative and playful nature of its design made me feel good about the amount of excitement and originality being created through this medium. Thus, the overall design of this website created a more enjoyable experience than one would have if they were visiting an average website. This made me want to continue exploring the website further. I think the site wants to convey that Luni is a brand that represents creativity and innovation. For me, there were only a few times when I felt confused by the site. Other than that, I felt both happy and inquisitive while exploring through Luni's website.
For me, the worst part about using this website is how much the different styles of text (color, size, and weight) plus the use of animations while scrolling were creating too many distractions to help me read them. I thought a few of these were visually interesting but at the same time made it difficult for me to concentrate on what I was trying to find out about the website. The size of the font was also an issue for me. This was a large size font so it really did provide a good visual impact; however, I had difficulty reading it comfortably. I enjoyed the website for its design; however, some of the design elements did detract from my overall experience on the site. I believe if the website did some work to provide a better readability experience, I would have enjoyed the website much more than I currently do.
The entirety of the site had a nice level of cohesion and felt very visually appealing in my opinion. Everything had a similar level of ownership, including colours, 3D components and page layout. The end result of the experience felt both well done and imaginative and thus made it enjoyable for me to look through. I also appreciated that the site was unlike your typical website in that it was more engaging, creating a better experience when exploring. There were some areas that I found distracting while using, but overall I found it to be a very satisfying experience due to its well-developed design identity. In general, I feel the website was able to successfully provide me with the impression it was trying to create.
